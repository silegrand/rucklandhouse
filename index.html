Our Story

A Building That
Remembers

Ruckland House has stood at the heart of Gravesend since the 1800s — first as a grand family home, then as a pillar of the local community. Acquired in 1905 and thoughtfully extended in the decades that followed, it carries within its walls the warmth of every gathering it has ever held.

Today, that heritage is your backdrop. The exposed original features, the high ceilings, the sense of occasion that no modern building can manufacture — it's all here, waiting for your story to be written inside it.

1800s
Origins The building first takes shape as a substantial family residence, its Victorian bones still visible in the fabric of the rooms today.
1905
Acquired Ruckland House comes under our stewardship, beginning its long journey as a venue for the Gravesend community.
1970s
Modernised A significant extension and modernisation brings the venue into a new era — blending mid-century structure with Victorian character.
Today
Ruckland House Now reborn under a name that honours its legacy, ready for another century of celebrations.
